Livia Foldes

Radical Art Theory posters

With the goal of making art theory and art history relevant, necessary, and accessible, this lecture series brings together writers, curators, and artists to discuss narratives of the historically marginalized. These digital posters, distributed via email, take advantage of their intimate scale, as a series of provocative questions intentionally overwhelms the small screen.

Becca Albee risograph print

Poster for a solo exhibition by New York-based artist Becca Albee that explores the relationship between color, therapy, and feminism in the twentieth century, including its problematic overlap with consumer culture.
